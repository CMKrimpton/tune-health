## Layer 1: Discovery

### 1A. SEO + Content (the article engine)

The AI newsroom already produces 2-3 articles/day at $0.06 each. This is our organic search engine.

**Why it works despite AI traffic decline:**
- Google traffic is down 33%, but the decline hits generic content hardest. Original investigation, opinion, and analysis survive because AI Overviews can't replicate them
- 91% of publishers are increasing investment in original investigations — we're already doing this at 1/100th the cost
- Each article targets long-tail health queries ("who funds AHA dietary guidelines", "seed oil industry research funding") that AI can't answer from training data

**What we do:**
- Pipeline produces articles optimized for search (structured data, meta tags, sitemap, RSS already built)
- Every article has subscribe CTA (newsletter) and app CTA (contextual to topic)
- "Follow the money" articles generate backlinks from health bloggers and independent researchers who share them
- Target: 500+ indexed pages within 6 months (currently 129 articles)

### 1B. Short-Form Video (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts)

Health content is one of the highest-performing categories on short-form video. 20-35 year olds discover health information on TikTok before Google.

**Format — "Follow the Money in 60 Seconds":**
- Take the most shareable finding from each article
- 60-second video: "The AHA says seed oils are heart-healthy. The AHA receives millions from seed oil companies. Here's what independent research actually shows."
- No face required initially — text overlay + b-roll + voiceover
- Link in bio → newsletter subscribe or article
- One video per article = 2-3 videos/day from existing content

**Why this works:**
- The "follow the money" angle is inherently viral — it triggers outrage and sharing
- Health debunking content consistently outperforms on TikTok
- Production cost: near zero (content already exists, video is repurposing)
- Authenticity > production value on these platforms

### 1C. Social Media (X/Twitter, Bluesky, Reddit)

**X/Twitter:**
- Thread each article's key findings: "THREAD: We looked into who funds the research that says seed oils are safe. Here's what we found."
- Share independence scores: "Our adversarial AI reviewer scored this article 8/10 for editorial independence. Here's what it flagged."
- Engage with health debates in real-time (the pinger already detects trending health topics)

**Reddit:**
- r/nutrition, r/health, r/science, r/supplements — share articles when directly relevant to active discussions
- Never spam. Only post when the article genuinely answers a question being asked
- Reddit drives high-intent traffic — people clicking from Reddit are already interested in the topic

**Bluesky:**
- Growing health/science community. Early mover advantage
- Same content strategy as X but with a more science-literate audience

### 1D. PR + Credibility

- Pitch "follow the money" findings to health journalists at STAT, NYT, WSJ — our investigations are genuinely newsworthy
- Independence scoring system is a story in itself: "This AI newsroom uses adversarial AI to score every article for industry bias"
- Target: one mainstream media mention in first 6 months

### 1E. App-Level: ASO (App Store Optimization)

Organic installs account for 65% of all app downloads. ASO is the single most cost-effective acquisition channel.

- **Keywords:** "health tracker", "lab results app", "AI health", "blood work analysis", "nutrition tracker AI"
- **Screenshots:** show the AI analyst in action — real conversations about real health data
- **Ratings:** target 4.6+ (30-50% conversion rate boost vs 4.2)
- **Localization:** Japanese + Korean markets routinely 2-3x downloads for health apps
- **Updates:** monthly ASO iteration — treat it as a living system

## Layer 3: Conversion (App)

### Contextual CTAs — Not "Download Our App"

Every touchpoint maps article topic to specific app feature:

| Content Topic | App CTA | Feature Highlighted |
|--------------|---------|-------------------|
| Seed oil / inflammation | "Track your omega-6/omega-3 ratio" | Lab OCR |
| Blood work / metabolic health | "Upload your labs — AI spots the red flags" | Lab analysis |
| Nutrition / diet | "Snap your meal — AI breaks down the evidence" | Meal analysis |
| Supplement evidence | "Check if your supplements actually work" | Evidence database |
| Sleep science | "Track sleep, mood, and nutrition correlations" | N=1 experiments |
| Longevity / biomarkers | "Build your personal health dashboard" | Health tracking |
| Mental health | "Correlate mood with nutrition and sleep" | Pattern detection |

This mapping already exists in the codebase (`src/utils/funnel.ts`). It powers the website CTAs and will power newsletter CTAs.

### In-App Referral Loop

Referred users show 37% higher retention and are 4x more likely to refer others.

- **Share health insights:** "Your omega-3 levels are in the 85th percentile" → share card → friend sees alumi Health branding
- **Invite system:** "Give 3 friends early access" — framed as invitation, not promotion
- **Milestone rewards:** 3 referrals → extended trial. 10 → premium feature unlock
- **Timing:** prompt sharing after positive health insights, not randomly

### Activation + Retention

Getting the download is only half the battle. Activation is what matters.

- **First-value moment:** under 60 seconds. User uploads a lab result photo → AI analysis appears immediately. This is the "aha" moment
- **Onboarding:** 3 screens max. Skip option on every screen. Don't block value with setup
- **Weekly health summary:** AI-generated, pushes users to re-engage. "Your inflammation markers improved 12% this month"
- **Content integration:** relevant alumi news articles surface inside the app based on user's health data. Reading an article about seed oils? If their omega-6 is high, the app says so

## Principles

1. **The app is the product.** Everything else is marketing. If a strategy doesn't eventually put someone in the app, cut it.

2. **Trust before conversion.** Never push the app before earning the reader's trust. The sequence is: value → habit → trust → conversion. Skip a step and you lose them.

3. **Content is the cheapest acquisition channel that exists.** At $0.06/article with indefinite shelf life, every article is an asset that compounds. Paid ads are expenses that evaporate.

4. **Follow the money — always.** This isn't just editorial philosophy. It's brand identity. It's what makes us shareable. It's what makes us impossible to copy. It's what makes people trust us enough to give us their health data.

5. **Build for 20-35 year olds.** They're the hardest to reach and the most valuable long-term. They'll use the app for decades. They share content. They distrust institutions. They're our people.
